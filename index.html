    • Igneous Rock Texture Table

    Texture of an igneous rock:

    - based primarily on crystal size - does NOT refer to the roughness or smoothness of the surface
    Pegmatitic - composed of very large crystals (larger than 2-3 cm).
    Phaneritic - composed of crystals which are large enough to see but smaller than pegmatitic texture, and the entire rock is composed of crystals.
    Aphanitic - a fine grained texture but the crystals are too small to see.
    Porphyritic - composed of crystals of two different sizes. Typically the large crystals (phenocrysts) are visible while the smaller crystal are not (referred to as groundmass).
    Glassy - rock is composed entirely of glass. Few, if any, crystals will be visible. -- the most readily recognized.
    Vesicula - The result is a "bubbly" appearance. formed when lava solidifies before gases are able to escape.
    Pyroclasti - composed of volcanic fragments. These fragments or clasts can be very fine (ash) or coarse (lapilli) or very coarse (bombs and blocks).
